## story design
1. Geb's story: environmental guilt, technological skepticism. dealing with overwhelming responsibility, pondering the path that led you (and your species) to where you are now. stoic, human. representation of life as a citizen in the [hegira]
2. Svaska's story: xenophobia, homesickness, (motherhood?). altruism across species. matter-of-fact. short, simple descriptions. sees and senses via heat and moisture, smell. representation of life as a species not closely connected to the [hegira]
3. [??]'s story (Or, My Girlfriend is a Robot): reality vs simulation. poetic writing and focus on feelings, colours.
4. [??]'s story: intrigue, mistrust. long, flamboyant words and descriptions. representation of politics, ideals and greater motions of the [hegira]
5. [??]'s story: written like a series of commands issued in a terminal. pain-ridden. colourless
6. Ooha-whiss' story: only observing (or at least commenting on) the slower changes. days pass like blinks. environmentalism putting the limited human experience to shame. explores the concept of narration where time speeds up as the story progresses, different *parts* of the narrator (e.g. a branch) can observe events faster than other parts, yet the experience is understood from the whole, perhaps as sensed feeling of urgency etc. Told as if from an elder reminiscing. the name translates to "observing/listening like wind" 

## setting concepts
- 1 species: 1 home planet. Typically each planet has one dominant species, and each [hegira] species has one home planet. This is because one species will naturally achieve dominance on a particular planet without intervention (and the [hegira] avoids interfering with non space-faring planets/species wherever possible), but when that species reaches space travel, the [hegira] will be quick to intervene and prevent them from endlessly expanding to other planets. species intermingle on various planets/satelites/space stations, but rarely have an "empire" of planets to their name
- species are diverse. Not just humanoids with fur/scales animal heads / tails, but actually diverse creatures, walking on all fours, needing their own biome e.g. temperature, air content. This can often make physical meetings inconvenient (e.g. for species with extreme biome requirements) so a lot of communication is done digitally
